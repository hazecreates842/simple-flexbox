When to plant. Plant daffodil bulbs in early autumn, ideally September, and potted bulbs in spring before they come into flower. You can also plant 'forced' bulbs indoors in September, such as the fragrant paper-white daffodil (Narcissus papyraceus)

A houseplant, sometimes known as a pot plant, potted plant, or an indoor plant, is an ornamental plant that is grown indoors. As such, they are found in places like residences and offices, mainly for decorative purposes.

Crossflower is native to Europe and Asia, and introduced to much of North America, excluding most of the east coast and southeastern regions. It is a rare visitor to New England, having been collected only in Massachusetts, but is widespread and highly invasive in Colorado, Nevada and Wyoming.

In 1991, the genus Coreopsis (Tickseed) was designated as Florida's Official State Wildflower in recognition of the colorful flowers used extensively in Florida's roadside plantings and highway beautification programs.
